Cepelinai

Dish Description

Cepelinai are potato dumplings made from grated and riced potatoes and stuffed with ground meat, dry curd cheese or mushrooms. It has been described as a national dish of Lithuania, and is typically served as a main dish.

The name of Cepelinai come from their shape that resembles a Zeppelin airship. Cepelinai are typically around 10 to 20 cm long, although the size depends on where they are made - in the western counties of Lithuania, Cepelinai are made bigger than in the east.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground pork, or an equal mix of pork, beef, and veal
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 8 large Idaho potatoes, peeled and finely grated, about 5 pounds
  • 2 large Idaho or Russet potatoes, about 1 1/2 pounds, peeled, boiled, and riced
  • 1 medium onion, finely grated
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, more as needed
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 pound bacon, diced
  • 1 large onion, coarsely chopped
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons milk, as needed
  • 1 lemon, juiced

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix together ground meat, finely chopped onion, the salt, a few grinds of pepper, and the egg until well combined.
  2. Cover and refrigerate until ready to use.
  3. Add the lemon juice to the raw grated potatoes so they don't turn brown.
  4. Place them in a fine-mesh cheesecloth or cotton dishtowel, and twist over a large bowl to get rid of the excess water.
  5. Pour off the water, reserving the potato starch at the bottom of the bowl.
  6. Unwrap the cheesecloth and place the raw potatoes in the bowl with the potato starch you reserved from the bottom of the bowl.
  7. Add the cooked riced potatoes, grated onion, and the salt, more to taste. Mix well.
  8. Put a large stockpot of well-salted water on to boil.
  9. To form the zeppelins, take about 1 cup of dumpling mixture and pat it flat in the palm of the hand.
  10. Place 1/4 cup or more of meat mixture in the center and, using slightly dampened hands, fold the potato mixture around the meat into a football shape, sealing well. Continue until both mixtures are used.
  11. Carefully lower dumplings (in batches if necessary to prevent crowding) into salted, boiling water to which 1 tablespoon of cornstarch has been added (to prevent dumplings from falling apart).
  12. Make sure water returns to the boil; reduce the heat to low and continue simmering until the filling is cooked through, 20 to 25 minutes.
  13. Remove dumplings with a slotted spoon or strainer, drain briefly on a clean dish towel and place on a heated platter.
  14. While the dumplings are boiling, make the gravy. In a medium skillet, fry the bacon and chopped onion until tender.
  15. Drain and combine with sour cream and black pepper. Thin with 1 to 2 tablespoons milk if necessary.
  16. Ladle the gravy over the dumplings or pass the gravy at the table.
